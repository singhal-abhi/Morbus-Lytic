PNEUMOTHORAX- Many of us are hearing it for the first time. In clinical field, detection of Pneumothorax often goes unnoticed, particularly, the exact location for treatment.


Pneumothorax is a medical condition characterized by air entering into the pleural space, which is the space between the lung and the chest wall. This condition can lead to partial or complete collapse of the affected lung. The pleural space is generally a thin fluid-filled space that helps the lungs expand and contract during breathing. The presence of air can disrupt the normal pressure balance between the lungs and the chest wall. This imbalance can cause the lung to collapse, leading to respiratory difficulties and other symptoms.

The incidence of PSP in males is observed to be 7.4 per 100,000 population per year in the United States. The incidence in females is observed as 1.2 per 100,000 population per 100,000 population per year in the United Kingdom. The reason for these geographic differences is unknown. Another hospital database study of emergency department visits from January 2008 to December 2014 reported that 79 percent of pneumothorax cases were males and 21 percent were females.
